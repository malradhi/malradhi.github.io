Biograph

Mohamed Daoudi is Professor of Computer Science at IMT Lille Douai and CRIStAL (UMR CNRS 9189). Mohamed Daoudi received Ph.D. degree in Computer Engineering from the University of Lille 1 in 1993 and Habilitation Ã  Diriger des Recherches from the University of Littoral in 2000. Mohamed Daoudi research interests include pattern recognition, computer vision, machine learning, biometrics, three-dimensional shape analysis, face recognition, facial expression recognition, action recognition and face and gesture for health applications. Mohamed Daoudi is the author of over 150 scientific publications that have appeared in international journals and conference proceedings. He has participated or has been the coordinator of many European and national projects in the areas of face shape analysis, 3D shape analysis, segmentation and retrieval. Mohamed Daoudi is an associate editor of Image, Vision and Computing Journal and IEEE Transactions On Multimedia. Mohamed Daoudi is a Fellow of IAPR, senior member IEEE and ACM member.

About

Hi, and welcome! I got a BSc degree in Computer Engineering at Basra University in 2007, and a MSc degree in Systems Engineering at Portsmouth University, UK which was achieved with first honors in 2012 and awarded the MSc top student certificate in 2013. I started from September 2016 to complete my PhD at the Speech Technology and Intelligent Interactions Laboratory in the Budapest University of Technology and Economics. I am working on designing vocoders and acoustic models for statistical speech synthesis. My main interest are the signal speech processing, acoustic models, voice conversion, deep learning, and machine lerning.
